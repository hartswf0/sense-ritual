How It Works

Practical Steps

1. Encounter: Swipe through film fragments. Right to collect, left to skip. Each clip is a potential agent in your sequence.

2. Compilation: Select a fragment from your collection. Choose a compiler strategy (Stanislavski, PlotDynamics, etc.) and compile it into an autonomous agent with its own rhetorical position, vulnerabilities, and decision logic.

3. Negotiation: Chat with the compiled agent. You have 5 turns to persuade it to join your film sequence. Reference its context, quote, tags, and temporal window. The agent evaluates your pitch based on narrative fit, thematic resonance, and temporal continuity.

4. Decision: After 5 turns, the agent decides: Join (matched) or Unmatch (rejected). Conviction builds or erodes based on your rhetorical moves.

Procedural Rhetoric

This is not a database query interface. It's a persuasive game where clips have agency. Each fragment becomes a procedural argument—you must sense its conceptual core and perform the ritual of persuasion through language.

The system uses concept-driven compilation: tags, quotes, and categories generate rhetorical positions (vulnerability, need, dimensional tensions, failure modes). You're not selecting clips; you're convincing autonomous agents to participate in your vision.

Sensing & Ritual

The interface demands attunement: read the fragment's essence, wound, and latent axes. The chat is a ritual space where you perform persuasion through grounded, specific language tied to the clip's anchors (quote text, named entities, time codes).

Generic praise fails. Vague pitches fail. You must sense the concept's vulnerability and speak to it directly.

About Sense Ritual

Theoretical Framework

Sense Ritual is an experimental interface for procedural rhetoric in film assembly. It reframes editorial selection as a persuasive game where clips are not passive assets but autonomous agents with rhetorical positions.

Drawing on Ian Bogost's concept of procedural rhetoric—the art of persuasion through rule-based systems—this tool treats film fragments as entities that must be convinced rather than commanded. The editor becomes a negotiator, not a curator.

Conceptual Mechanics

Each fragment is compiled into an agent with:

  • Identity: Essence (core concept), Wound (vulnerability), Need (abstract drive), Voice (tonal quality)
  • Decision Logic: Trust conditions, doubt triggers, repair mechanisms
  • Latent Axes: Dimensional tensions (e.g., Pattern-Seeking vs. Paranoia, Control vs. Resistance)
  • Failure Cases: Concrete pitch failures that trigger rejection

These are not arbitrary. They're generated from the fragment's semantic context: tags, quotes, category, temporal position. The system performs concept-driven cultivation—every rhetorical position grows from the clip's inherent properties.

Why Persuasion?

Traditional NLEs treat clips as inert. You drag, drop, trim. But clips carry conceptual weight—they have thematic commitments, narrative implications, rhetorical force. By making clips into agents, Sense Ritual externalizes the editorial decision-making process as dialogue.

You must sense what the clip wants (its Need), what threatens it (its Wound), and how to speak to its core. The 5-turn negotiation is a ritual—a structured performance of editorial judgment.

Compiler Strategies

Different compilers generate different rhetorical frames:

  • Stanislavski: Psychological realism, character motivation, emotional truth
  • PlotDynamics: Structural position, narrative function, causal links
  • StateBureaucracy: Procedural logic, jurisdictional boundaries, permissions
  • MagicCompiler07: Hypotheticals, counterfactuals, "Magic If" scenarios

Choose the compiler that matches your editorial stance. Each produces a different agent personality.
