# GARDEN - A New Video Editing Paradigm

## The Problem with Traditional Video Editing

Traditional non-linear editors (NLEs) treat video editing as **assembly**:
- Drag clips onto timeline
- Scrub playhead left-right
- Cut, trim, arrange sequentially
- Think in temporal coordinates (00:00:12:15)

This is **manufacturing**, not **discovery**.

---

## GARDEN: Video Editing as Gameplay

### Core Innovation: **Spatial → Temporal**

Instead of thinking in time, think in **space**:
- **Left to right** = shot order
- **Block width** = shot duration (1 second per block)
- **Vertical position** = when it enters the sequence
- **Gameplay rhythm** = editorial rhythm

### The Five Paradigm Shifts

#### 1. **Gameplay as Interface**
- Every Tetris move is an editorial decision
- Rotate = choose different clip
- Position = determine order
- Drop = commit to the cut
- **Your muscle memory becomes editing memory**

#### 2. **Freeze-Frame Mosaic**
- Videos play while pieces fall
- **Moment of impact** = frame capture
- Texture freezes, becomes image
- Grid accumulates visual collage
- Export as perfect A4 portrait PNG (1080×1920)

#### 3. **Ring Buffer Memory**
- Each video tracks its playback position
- First use: 0-2 seconds
- Second use: 2-4 seconds (continues!)
- Loops at 10 seconds
- **No repeats until all 54 videos shown**
- Natural variation without randomness

#### 4. **Kinetic Editing**
- Ghost wall magnifies current video
- Camera smoothly follows attention
- Sound design rewards every action:
  - Move: 300Hz slide
  - Rotate: 450Hz chirp  
  - Lock: 250Hz thud
  - Clear: 800Hz reward
  - Focus/unfocus: 600/400Hz pings
- Touch blocks to inspect frozen frames

#### 5. **Procedural Montage**
Two playback modes:
- **Sequence**: Your edit (1-10s segments, ring buffer cuts)
- **Full**: All source videos (see raw material)
- **EDL export**: Industry-standard edit decision list
- Timeline strips show each segment with metadata

## The Philosophy

> **"This is foraging, not farming.  
> This is gardening, not construction.  
> This is discovery, not assembly."**

Traditional editing assumes you know what you want. You **manufacture** a vision.

GARDEN assumes you **discover** what emerges. You **cultivate** possibilities.

### Foraging vs Farming
- **Farming**: Plant exactly what you want, control everything
- **Foraging**: Explore landscape, take what appears, adapt

### Gardening vs Construction  
- **Construction**: Blueprint → materials → assembly → building
- **Gardening**: Seeds → conditions → tending → growth

### Discovery vs Assembly
- **Assembly**: Known parts → predetermined structure
- **Discovery**: Unknown territory → emergent patterns

## Why This Works

1. **Removes creative paralysis**: No blank timeline to fill
2. **Engages different brain regions**: Motor cortex, not just prefrontal
3. **Creates happy accidents**: Gameplay creates unexpected juxtapositions
4. **Makes editing playful**: Game first, tool second
5. **Produces unique results**: Every session = different montage

### The Ring Buffer Secret

Traditional shuffle = chaos (same clip might repeat immediately)

Ring buffer = **structured variety**:
- Guarantees all videos used before repeating
- Each video continues from where it left off
- Creates natural narrative flow
- Feels intentional, not random

### The Freeze-Frame Magic

Instead of "which frame should I use?" → **the game decides**
- Moment of lock = moment of capture
- No scrubbing, no choosing
- Gameplay timing = frame selection
- Fast drops = action frames
- Slow falls = contemplative frames
